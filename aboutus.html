FUN FACTS


  • Oxford University Press even created a special Roald Dahl Dictionary, featuring almost 8,000 real and imaginary words which he loved to use.

  • James and the Giant Peach was originally going to be called James and the Giant Cherry. It was changed from a cherry to a peach because Dahl said a peach is “prettier, bigger and squishier than a cherry.”

  • Roald Dahl was a spy, an ace fighter pilot, a chocolate historian and a medical inventor.

  • He invented more than 500 new words and character names.

  • Roald Dahl said his favourite book was The BFG, but he also particularly liked Danny the Champion of the World.

  • He remains for many the world’s No. 1 storyteller

  • He enlisted in the Royal Air Force at 23 years old.

  • Roald Dahl died on 23 November 1990, aged 74. He was buried with some of his favourite things.

  • Many of Dahl’s characters were based on people he’d met in real life

  • Roald Dahl wrote for around 4 hours every day. His writing schedule was said to be from 10 am to 12 pm and from 4 pm to 6 pm every single day.
